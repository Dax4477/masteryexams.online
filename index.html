1. Kerala History & Renaissance: The Backbone of KPSC

For any exam conducted by the Kerala PSC, the history of Kerala and the Renaissance movement forms a substantial portion of the syllabus. MasteryExams provides detailed notes and quizzes on the following key areas:

Key Social Reformers

The transformation of Kerala from a caste-ridden society to a model of social progress was led by visionary leaders.
Sree Narayana Guru (1856–1928): Known for his slogan "One Caste, One Religion, One God for Man." His consecration of the Shiva idol at Aruvippuram in 1888 challenged the Brahminical monopoly on temple rituals. He founded the SNDP Yogam in 1903. His works like Atmopadesa Satakam and Daiva Dasakam are frequently asked in exams.
Ayyankali (1863–1941): The leader of the Pulayas. He organized the famous Villuvandi Samaram (Bullock Cart Strike) in 1893 to assert the right to use public roads. He founded the Sadhu Jana Paripalana Sangham in 1907.
Chattampi Swamikal: A contemporary of Narayana Guru who worked for the reformation of the Nair community. His work Pracheena Malayalam critiques the caste system based on Vedas.
V.T. Bhattathiripad: A revolutionary in the Namboodiri community. His play Adukkalayil Ninnu Arangathekk (From the Kitchen to the Stage) was instrumental in changing the lives of Namboodiri women.

Major Revolts and Movements

Aspirants must study the timeline of resistance against British rule and feudalism.
Attingal Revolt (1721): Often cited as the first organized rebellion against the British in Kerala.
Pazhassi Raja's Revolts: The Cotiote War. Pazhassi Raja used guerilla warfare against the British in the forests of Wayanad.
Vaikom Satyagraha (1924): The first systematic agitation for the right of lower castes to walk on the roads surrounding the Vaikom temple. It was supported by Mahatma Gandhi and Periyar E.V. Ramasamy.
Guruvayur Satyagraha (1931): Led by K. Kelappan (Kerala Gandhi) demanding temple entry for all Hindus. This eventually led to the Temple Entry Proclamation.

2. Indian Constitution: The Law of the Land

Polity is a high-scoring subject if understood correctly. Unlike history, facts here do not change often (except for amendments). MasteryExams breaks down the constitution into digestible modules.

Must-Study Articles for PSC:
- Article 14: Equality before law.
- Article 17: Abolition of Untouchability.
- Article 21A: Right to Education.
- Article 32: Constitutional Remedies (Heart and Soul of Constitution).
- Article 40: Organization of Village Panchayats.
- Article 51A: Fundamental Duties.

We also cover the Preamble, often called the Identity Card of the Constitution. It declares India as a Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic. The 42nd Amendment (1976), known as the Mini-Constitution, added the words 'Socialist', 'Secular', and 'Integrity'. Understanding the Schedules (1 to 12) is also vital, especially Schedule 8 (Languages) and Schedule 11 & 12 (Panchayats and Municipalities).

3. General Science: The Deciding Factor

In recent LDC exams, science questions (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) have become tougher, often based directly on SCERT textbooks from Class 8, 9, and 10. Our study notes are aligned with these texts.

Biology

Focus areas include Human Anatomy (Heart, Brain, Eye), Vitamins and Deficiency Diseases (e.g., Vitamin A - Night Blindness, Vitamin C - Scurvy), and Lifestyle Diseases. Recent exams also ask about environmental science, including the food chain, ecosystem, and biodiversity hotspots like the Western Ghats.

Physics & Chemistry

Candidates must master concepts of Motion (Newton's Laws), Light (Reflection, Refraction, Lenses), and Electricity (Ohm's Law, Power, Transformers). In Chemistry, the Periodic Table, Acids and Bases (pH scale), and common chemical names (e.g., Baking Soda is Sodium Bicarbonate) are recurring topics.

Current affairs can be overwhelming. Mastery Exams uses a "Filter Method" to identify what is exam-relevant. We categorize news into:

5. Mathematics & Mental Ability

For many, this is the hardest section. However, PSC maths is repetitive. We provide shortcuts for:

Time and Work: If A does a job in x days and B in y days, together they take xy/(x+y).
Calendar: Calculating the day of the week for a given date.
Series Completion: Finding the missing number in AP/GP or logical sequences.
Clock: Finding the angle between hour and minute hands using the formula |30H - 5.5M|.

6. English & Malayalam Grammar

Language proficiency carries 20 marks in most exams (10 English + 10 Malayalam).
English: Focus on Tenses, Prepositions (in, on, at), Articles (a, an, the), Phrasal Verbs (give up, call off), and One Word Substitutes.
Malayalam: Sandhi (Loapsandhi, Agamasandhi), Samasam, Pariyayam (Synonyms), and Vipareetham (Antonyms). Famous literary works and pen names (e.g., Vaikom Muhammad Basheer as Beypore Sultan) are also important.

7. Exam Strategy for 2025

Consistency is Key: It is better to study for 2 hours every day than for 12 hours once a week. Use our Daily Quizzes to keep your momentum.

Mock Tests: Take full-length mock tests under timed conditions. Analyze your mistakes. If you got a question on "Rivers of India" wrong, go back and study that entire topic, not just that one question.
